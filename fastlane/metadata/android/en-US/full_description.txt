Throttling VPN App is a developer tool that simulates poor network conditions by throttling traffic for specific apps via a local VPN.

Select target apps and control their network speed from 50 kbps (2G) to 5 Mbps (HSPA+) using a logarithmic speed slider. The app uses a token bucket algorithm to accurately limit throughput, powered by a Go-based tun2socks engine with the gVisor TCP/IP stack.

<b>Features:</b>

* <b>Speed control</b> — Adjustable from 50 kbps to 5 Mbps with a logarithmic slider
* <b>Three modes</b> — Block (blackhole), Throttle (rate-limited), Unlimited (passthrough)
* <b>Per-app targeting</b> — Choose which apps are affected
* <b>Real-time adjustment</b> — Change speed without restarting the VPN
* <b>Persistent app list</b> — Target apps are saved automatically

<b>How it works:</b>

Traffic from selected apps is captured via a TUN interface, passed through a token bucket throttle layer written in Go, and forwarded through the tun2socks/gVisor network stack. This provides accurate and reliable bandwidth limiting at the network level.

<b>Use cases:</b>

* Test how your app behaves on slow networks
* Simulate 2G/3G conditions for QA and debugging
* Verify offline/timeout handling in your applications

This app requires VPN permission to function. No data is collected or sent to external servers — all traffic processing happens locally on your device.
